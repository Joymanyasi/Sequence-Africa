Initiative

Sequence Africa

Africa generates less than 3% of the world's genomic data — despite carrying the greatest burden of infectious and neglected tropical diseases. Sequence Africa exists to change that.

Through the Substack newsletter and the NGS Hub repository, I document African sequencing infrastructure, advocate for genomic data equity, and build resources for researchers working with limited access to bioinformatics training.

Background

About Joy

I'm a molecular biologist and NGS data analyst based in Nairobi, Kenya, with an MSc in Biotechnology and a research background spanning vector biology, transcriptomics, and clinical genomics.

My first-author publication in PLOS Neglected Tropical Diseases (2020) used RNA-Seq to characterise chemosensory gene expression in tsetse fly — the vector of African trypanosomiasis. That work gave me deep hands-on experience with differential expression pipelines and the biological interpretation of complex sequencing datasets.

Since then I've extended into clinical NGS — building germline and somatic variant calling pipelines for diagnostic and research applications — and into cancer genomics, where identifying actionable mutations has direct therapeutic implications.

Alongside technical work, I'm committed to building genomics capacity in Africa. Through the Genomics & Biotechnology Workgroup of the African Bioethics Network and Sequence Africa, I work on the infrastructure, ethics, and knowledge commons that African researchers need to lead their own genomic futures.
